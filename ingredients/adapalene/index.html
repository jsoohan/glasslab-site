Modified adapalene 0.1%/benzoyl peroxide 2.5% regimens (every-other-night or with moisturizer) improved local tolerability during the first four weeks without compromising efficacy at Week 12, achieving up to 67% total lesion count reduction across all groups.
A randomized investigator-blind parallel-group study to assess efficacy and safety of azelaic acid 15% gel vs. adapalene 0.1% gel in the treatment and maintenance treatment of female adult acne
PMID: 25399481 · RCT · positive · topical
Azelaic acid 15% gel demonstrated non-inferior efficacy to adapalene 0.1% gel in controlling inflammatory female adult acne over 9 months, with significantly lower dryness and scaling side effects compared to adapalene.
Effect of sequential application of topical adapalene and clindamycin phosphate in the treatment of Japanese patients with acne vulgaris
PMID: 21254867 · RCT · positive · topical
Sequential application of topical adapalene and clindamycin phosphate significantly improved acne severity, lesion counts, and quality of life in Japanese patients, with weekly maintenance application of adapalene being as effective as daily application.
A randomized controlled trial of combination treatment with ketoconazole 2% cream and adapalene 0.1% gel in pityriasis versicolor
PMID: 24802530 · RCT · positive · topical
Combination therapy with adapalene 0.1% gel and ketoconazole 2% cream was more effective and faster-acting than ketoconazole monotherapy in treating pityriasis versicolor, with a statistically significant difference at Week 4.
[Comparative study of the efficacy and tolerability of 0.1 and 0.03 p.100 adapalene gel and 0.025 p.100 tretinoin gel in the treatment of acne]
PMID: 8761775 · RCT · positive · topical
0.1% adapalene gel demonstrated equivalent efficacy to 0.025% tretinoin gel in treating acne over 12 weeks while showing superior skin tolerability, with less erythema, dryness, scaling, and burning upon application.
