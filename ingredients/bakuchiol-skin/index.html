분석된 논문 (7편)

Prospective, randomized, double-blind assessment of topical bakuchiol and retinol for facial photoageing
PMID: 29947134 · RCT · positive · topical
Bakuchiol 0.5% cream applied twice daily was comparable to retinol 0.5% cream in reducing wrinkle surface area and hyperpigmentation over 12 weeks, while demonstrating better tolerability with less scaling and stinging than retinol.
A Randomized Controlled Trial of the Genistein Plus Bakuchiol and Vitamins (GEN+) Product for Male Facial Skin: Effects on Skin Appearance and Properties
PMID: 41562471 · 2024 · RCT · positive · topical
A 12-week twice-daily application of the GEN+ (Genistein plus Bakuchiol and Vitamins) product in men aged 45-65 significantly improved cheek skin lightening, color consistency, and forehead spot reduction compared to placebo, with mild and self-resolving adverse events.
Clinical Evaluation of a Nature-Based Bakuchiol Anti-Aging Moisturizer for Sensitive Skin
PMID: 33346506 · 2020 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A nature-based bakuchiol cleanser and moisturizer used twice daily for 4 weeks was well tolerated and effective in sensitive skin subjects, demonstrating statistically significant improvements in visual smoothness, radiance, overall appearance, global anti-aging, and a 16% increase in skin moisture content.
Silybum marianum Extract: A Highly Effective Natural Alternative to Retinoids to Prevent Skin Aging Without Side Effects
PMID: 39692756 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Silybum marianum extract (SME) demonstrated superior collagen III and hyaluronic acid production compared to bakuchiol and retinol in skin explants, and showed stronger anti-wrinkle effects than bakuchiol with a significantly better safety profile than retinol in a 56-day placebo-controlled clinical trial.
Efficacy and safety of a 3-in-1 antiaging night facial serum containing melatonin, bakuchiol, and ascorbyl tetraisopalmitate through clinical and histological analysis
PMID: 32061043 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A 3-in-1 night facial serum containing melatonin, bakuchiol, and ascorbyl tetraisopalmitate applied nightly for 12–24 weeks produced clinically and statistically significant improvements in skin texture, pigmentation, erythema, tone, and wrinkles, with effects becoming more pronounced at 24 weeks.
Trichloroacetic acid model to accurately capture the efficacy of treatments for postinflammatory hyperpigmentation
PMID: 32253506 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Topical bakuchiol showed statistically significant improvement in TCA-induced postinflammatory hyperpigmentation compared to vehicle and control, and a greater but non-statistically significant improvement in acne-induced PIH, suggesting it may reduce PIH severity.
An Open Label Clinical Trial to Evaluate the Efficacy and Tolerance of a Retinol and Vitamin C Facial Regimen in Women With Mild-to-Moderate Hyperpigmentation and Photodamaged Facial Skin
PMID: 27050703 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A 12-week regimen of 0.5% encapsulated retinol (with bakuchiol) and 30% vitamin C moisturizer produced statistically significant improvements in hyperpigmentation and photodamage parameters at weeks 8 and 12, with transient dryness resolving by week 12.
