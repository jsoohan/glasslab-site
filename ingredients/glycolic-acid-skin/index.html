Effectiveness of water as the neutralising agent for glycolic acid peels in skin phototypes IV-V
PMID: 33070326 · RCT · positive · topical
Water is an effective substitute for sodium bicarbonate plus solution as a neutralising agent for 35% glycolic acid chemical peels in skin phototypes IV-V, with no significant differences in erythema or pruritus, though a significant difference in pain scores was observed immediately post-neutralisation.

PMID: 18452434 · RCT · positive · topical
Topical application of a combination of retinol, lactose, and glycolic acid twice daily for 12 weeks significantly improved photodamaged skin appearance as measured by both clinical assessment and objective instrumental measurements of skin microrelief and moisturization.

PMID: 10906645 · RCT · mixed · topical
Short-term topical application of 10% glycolic acid increased UVB-induced skin tanning in both Asian and Caucasian subjects and UVA-induced tanning in Asian subjects, without affecting pre-existing light-induced pigmentation, suggesting sunscreen inclusion in AHA products may be warranted.
