분석된 논문 (12편)

Efficacy and safety of topical agents in the treatment of melasma: What's evidence? A systematic review and meta-analysis
PMID: 36566490 · 2021 · meta-analysis · positive · topical
Kojic acid demonstrated statistically significant efficacy in reducing melasma severity (SMD -0.9) with the lowest incidence of skin irritation (5.3%) among evaluated topical agents, supporting it as a safe and effective non-hydroquinone alternative.
A Bayesian network meta-analysis of 14 molecules inhibiting UV daylight-induced pigmentation
PMID: 38433524 · meta-analysis · positive · topical
2-MNG 0.5% ranked first among 14 anti-pigmenting agents including kojic acid 1% and hydroquinone 4% in a Bayesian network meta-analysis, demonstrating immediate and persistent efficacy against UV daylight-induced pigmentation from end of irradiation through 1-month follow-up.
Inhibition of Human Tyrosinase Requires Molecular Motifs Distinctively Different from Mushroom Tyrosinase
PMID: 29427586 · other · mixed · topical
Kojic acid showed weak inhibition of human tyrosinase (IC50 > 500 μmol/L), while the newly identified Thiamidol demonstrated potent and selective human tyrosinase inhibition (IC50 = 1.1 μmol/L) with clinical reduction of age spots within 4 weeks, highlighting that mushroom tyrosinase-based screening poorly predicts human efficacy.
Evaluation of a kojic acid, emblica extract, and glycolic acid formulation compared with hydroquinone 4% for skin lightening
PMID: 21049734 · RCT · positive · topical
A topical formulation combining kojic acid, emblica extract, and glycolic acid demonstrated efficacy parity with prescription hydroquinone 4% cream for treating mild to moderate facial dyschromia over 12 weeks.
Treatment of melasma using kojic acid in a gel containing hydroquinone and glycolic acid
PMID: 10417583 · RCT · positive · topical
Adding 2% kojic acid to a gel containing 10% glycolic acid and 2% hydroquinone significantly improved melasma clearance compared to the gel without kojic acid, with 60% vs 47.5% of patients achieving greater than 50% clearance.
The Efficacy of Topical Cosmetic Containing Alpha-Arbutin 5% and Kojic Acid 2% Compared With Triple Combination Cream for the Treatment of Melasma: A Split-Face, Evaluator-Blinded Randomized Pilot Stu
PMID: 39555866 · 2023 · RCT · positive · topical
Alpha-arbutin 5% and kojic acid 2% cream showed comparable efficacy to triple combination cream for melasma treatment, with significantly lower recurrence rates and fewer adverse effects (erythema and stinging) than the gold standard therapy.
Effect of a Tranexamic Acid, Kojic Acid, and Niacinamide Containing Serum on Facial Dyschromia: A Clinical Evaluation
PMID: 31141852 · 2019 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A topical serum containing 3% tranexamic acid, 1% kojic acid, and 5% niacinamide significantly improved PIH, melasma, hyperpigmentation, skin texture, and skin tone homogeneity beginning at week 2 and continuing through week 12, with significant reduction in melanin index by week 12.
Evaluation of the of the efficacy of Fractional Erbium-Doped Yttrium Aluminum Garnet Laser-Assisted Drug Delivery of Kojic Acid in the Treatment of Melasma; A split face, comparative clinical study
PMID: 34407729 · clinical_trial · positive · mixed
Fractional Er:YAG laser-assisted delivery of topical kojic acid produced statistically significant greater improvement in melasma than kojic acid cream alone, as measured by MASI score, physician assessment, and patient satisfaction.
An effective hydroquinone alternative for topical skin lightening
PMID: 33098161 · RCT · positive · topical
TFC-1067, a gem difluorocompound tyrosinase inhibitor, produced statistically significant lightening of facial dyschromia comparable to 2% hydroquinone after 8-12 weeks without causing unwanted lightening of normal surrounding skin or melanocyte toxicity.
4-n-butylresorcinol, a highly effective tyrosinase inhibitor for the topical treatment of hyperpigmentation
PMID: 23205541 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
4-n-butylresorcinol demonstrated far superior tyrosinase inhibition (IC50 of 21 μmol/L) compared to kojic acid, arbutin, and hydroquinone, and significantly reduced age spot appearance in vivo within 8 weeks of twice-daily topical application.
Assessment of topical hypopigmenting agents on solar lentigines of Asian women
PMID: 12077522 · clinical_trial · negative · topical
A formulation containing 5% ascorbyl glucosamine, 1% kojic acid, and alpha-hydroxyacid esters was found inefficacious on solar lentigines in Asian women, while a stabilized soy extract showed a modest lightening effect.
Analytic assessment under ultraviolet light of actinic lentigines under bleaching treatment
PMID: 21649815 · RCT · positive · topical
A topical combination of glycolic acid, kojic acid, lipohydroxyacid, and Vitreoscilla extract demonstrated a progressive bleaching effect on actinic lentigines, detectable as early as 1 month and increasing over 3 months of daily application when assessed via ultraviolet light-enhanced visualization.
