Combination LED therapy using 633 nm and 830 nm wavelengths produced significant improvements in facial skin profilometry metrics at week 12, with 52% of subjects showing 25-50% improvement in photoaging scores and 81% reporting significant periorbital wrinkle improvement.
Regulation of skin collagen metabolism in vitro using a pulsed 660 nm LED light source: clinical correlation with a single-blinded study
PMID: 19587693 · clinical_trial · positive · LED
Pulsed 660 nm LED therapy increased type-1 procollagen by 31% and decreased MMP-1 by 18% in reconstructed skin, with over 90% of treated individuals showing reduced wrinkle depth and surface roughness in a split-face clinical study.
Comparative effects of exposure to different light sources (He-Ne laser, InGaAl diode laser, a specific type of noncoherent LED) on skin blood flow for the head
PMID: 8914685 · clinical_trial · mixed · device
InGaAl diode laser irradiation at low fluences (0.12-0.36 J/cm²) increased skin blood flow by 54%, while non-coherent LED monochromatic light at higher fluences (0.68-1.36 J/cm²) decreased blood flow by 36%, suggesting blood flow changes are more dependent on radiant exposure than light coherency.
Photodynamic therapy for multiple skin cancers
PMID: 3059383 · clinical_trial · negative · device
Photodynamic therapy using hematoporphyrin derivative and laser light for multiple skin cancers produced poor results in a pilot clinical study, leading to abandonment of a planned full clinical trial on ethical grounds.
Low-irradiance red LED traffic lamps as light source in PDT for actinic keratoses
PMID: 23126296 · clinical_trial · positive · LED
Red LED traffic lamps used as light source in ALA-PDT effectively treated actinic keratoses in 15 patients, with 13 achieving satisfactory remission after a single session and minimal pain reported.
LED photoprevention: reduced MED response following multiple LED exposures
PMID: 18306161 · clinical_trial · positive · LED
Pre-treatment with 660nm LED light therapy significantly reduced UV-B induced erythema in 85% of subjects, demonstrating an SPF-15-like protective effect and reduced post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.
Photodynamic therapy for rosacea in Chinese patients
PMID: 30118905 · clinical_trial · positive · mixed
ALA-PDT using topical 5% aminolevulinic acid combined with LED red light (635nm) achieved complete resolution of inflammatory lesions and subjective symptoms in all 20 Chinese rosacea patients after 24 weeks with only transient, tolerable side effects.
Enhanced effectiveness of photodynamic therapy with laser light fractionation in patients with esophageal cancer
PMID: 9255531 · clinical_trial · mixed · device
Fractionated laser light delivery during photodynamic therapy with hematoporphyrin sensitization improved treatment effectiveness in esophageal cancer patients, achieving complete remission in all three early-cancer patients, though with increased mild esophageal stenosis as a side effect.
