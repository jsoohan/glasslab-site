Enhancing Facial Rejuvenation Outcomes With a Novel Retinaldehyde-Based Cream: A Comparative Randomized Intra-Individual Study
PMID: 41312593 · RCT · positive · topical
A retinaldehyde, niacinamide, and haritaki fruit extract cream applied daily to one hemiface significantly improved wrinkles, firmness, plumpness, texture, skin tone homogeneity, and radiance versus the untreated control hemiface as early as 1 month post-rejuvenation procedure.
Antibacterial activity of retinaldehyde against Propionibacterium acnes
PMID: 10473957 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Topical 0.05% retinaldehyde demonstrated direct antibacterial activity against P. acnes with MICs of 4-8 mg/l in vitro and a significant reduction in viable P. acnes counts (median 10² log/cm²) after 2 weeks of daily application in vivo, far outperforming retinoic acid (MIC >128 mg/l).
Topical retinaldehyde on human skin: biologic effects and tolerance
PMID: 7798613 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Topical retinaldehyde demonstrated dose-dependent biologic activity on human skin, including increased epidermal thickness, keratinocyte proliferation, and differentiation markers, while being well tolerated at 0.1% and 0.05% concentrations for prolonged use up to 3 years.
Combined 0.1% retinaldehyde/ 6% glycolic acid cream in prophylaxis and treatment of acne scarring
PMID: 17377389 · RCT · positive · topical
A 0.1% retinaldehyde/6% glycolic acid cream applied nightly for 3 months significantly improved acne scarring, erythema, hyperpigmentation, and inflammatory lesions compared to vehicle in compliant patients with moderate acne.
Clinical use of topical retinaldehyde on photoaged skin
PMID: 10473961 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Topical retinaldehyde was found to be both effective and well tolerated in improving signs of photoaging on human skin.
The efficacy and safety of multilamellar vesicle containing retinaldehyde: A double-blinded, randomized, split-face controlled study
PMID: 33569865 · RCT · positive · topical
Multilamellar vesicle-encapsulated retinaldehyde (0.05% and 0.1%) significantly improved wrinkle depth, skin hydration, elasticity, and facial contour after 8 weeks, outperforming retinol at equivalent concentrations with no adverse events.
Profilometric evaluation of photodamage after topical retinaldehyde and retinoic acid treatment
PMID: 9843009 · RCT · positive · topical
Retinaldehyde 0.05% cream significantly reduced wrinkle and roughness features in photodamaged skin comparable to retinoic acid 0.05%, but with superior tolerability and fewer local irritation side effects.
Efficacy and safety of retinaldehyde 0.1% and 0.05% creams used to treat photoaged skin: A randomized double-blind controlled trial
PMID: 29663701 · RCT · positive · topical
Both RAL 0.1% and 0.05% creams significantly improved skin texture, hydration, and reduced TEWL in photoaged skin over 3 months, with only the 0.1% concentration additionally improving the melanin index, and both concentrations well tolerated with good safety profiles.
Antiaging efficacy of a retinaldehyde-based cream compared with glycolic acid peel sessions: A randomized controlled study
PMID: 30027612 · RCT · positive · mixed
A daily-applied 0.1% retinaldehyde cream (with GGO and Pre-tocopheryl) was as effective as three sequential glycolic acid peels (20–70%) in reducing crow's feet wrinkle depth and length, superior in refining skin texture, and associated with 12-fold fewer adverse events over 8 weeks in women with photoaged skin.
Efficacy and safety of 0.1% retinaldehyde/ 6% glycolic acid (diacneal) for mild to moderate acne vulgaris. A multicentre, double-blind, randomized, vehicle-controlled trial
PMID: 15724103 · RCT · positive · topical
A topical combination of 0.1% retinaldehyde and 6% glycolic acid (Diacneal) significantly reduced papules, pustules, and comedones from month 1 onwards and demonstrated superior global improvement over vehicle in mild to moderate acne vulgaris, with good tolerability.
Tolerance profile of retinol, retinaldehyde and retinoic acid under maximized and long-term clinical conditions
PMID: 10473963 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Retinaldehyde and retinol demonstrated significantly better skin tolerance profiles compared to retinoic acid, with far lower rates of erythema, scaling, and burning/pruritus in both maximized patch testing and long-term 44-week clinical use.
Tolerance of topical retinaldehyde in humans
PMID: 10473964 · review · positive · topical
Topical retinaldehyde demonstrated excellent long-term tolerance on human skin, including sensitive facial skin, with no phototoxic, photo-allergic, or significant side effects observed over 12-89 months of use.
The antibacterial activity of topical retinoids: the case of retinaldehyde
PMID: 12218231 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Retinaldehyde (0.05%) demonstrated significant in vitro antibacterial activity against gram-positive bacteria and significantly reduced bacterial counts (P. acnes and staphylococci) on human skin in vivo after topical application, with no resistant bacteria emerging after 2 weeks of use.

PMID: 25078981 · RCT · positive · topical
Retinaldehyde-based cream applied daily for 3 months produced a measurable lightening effect (ΔL* = 2) on solar lentigines compared to control, confirmed by both clinical evaluation and calibrated colour imaging analysis.

PMID: 15370406 · RCT · positive · mixed
Daily topical application of 0.05% retinaldehyde significantly potentiated non-ablative 1540nm Er:glass laser remodeling, resulting in greater dermal thickness increases (5.27% vs 1.13% forehead; 10.54% vs 3.57% neck) compared to laser with excipient alone.
Clinical efficacy and safety of a topical combination of retinaldehyde 0.1% with erythromycin 4% in acne vulgaris
PMID: 10564319 · RCT · positive · topical
Retinaldehyde 0.1% combined with erythromycin 4% significantly improved comedones and microcysts in acne vulgaris compared to erythromycin alone, with very satisfactory local tolerance and only minor irritation reported.
Limited appearance of apocarotenoids is observed in plasma after consumption of tomato juices: a randomized human clinical trial
PMID: 30239552 · clinical_trial · neutral · oral
After consuming carotenoid-rich tomato juices, β-apo-13-carotenone was the only apocarotenoid consistently quantifiable in plasma of all subjects, reaching levels comparable to all-trans-retinoic acid, while retinal and other apocarotenoids were largely absent or undetectable, suggesting poor absorption or rapid clearance.
Acne rosacea: effectiveness of 532 nm laser on the cosmetic appearance of the skin
PMID: 20470675 · RCT · positive · mixed
532 nm laser combined with topical retinaldehyde improved overall redness, telangiectasia, and skin texture in acne rosacea patients to a greater degree than laser treatment alone.
Retinaldehyde/hyaluronic acid fragments: a synergistic association for the management of skin aging
PMID: 21649816 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Daily application of retinaldehyde (0.05%) combined with intermediate-size hyaluronate fragments (0.5% or 1%) for 90 days significantly improved overall photoaging scores, reduced wrinkles (up to 34% for perioral wrinkles), and improved clinical signs of aging including elasticity, hyperpigmentation, and ptosis, with good tolerability.

PMID: 15724104 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A combination of retinaldehyde 0.1% and glycolic acid 6% (Diacneal) demonstrated excellent tolerance and significant reduction in both inflammatory and retentional acne lesions over 90 days when used alongside other anti-acne treatments such as benzoyl peroxide and topical antibiotics.
Efficacy of topical 0.05% retinaldehyde in skin aging by ultrasound and rheological techniques

Topical 0.05% retinaldehyde applied over one year significantly increased epidermal thickness and cutaneous elasticity compared to control, with trends toward increased dermal thickness and reduced stiffness, suggesting anti-aging efficacy.

PMID: 26682557 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A novel cream containing nicotinamide 4%, arbutin 3%, bisabolol 1%, and retinaldehyde 0.05% significantly reduced MASI scores and melasma surface area with good tolerance, safety, and patient satisfaction in epidermal melasma treatment.

PMID: 41735774 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A biomimetic exosome-encapsulated retinal formulation applied daily for 12 weeks significantly improved erythema, skin tone, texture, and fine lines/wrinkles in all 20 participants with mild-to-moderate facial photodamage, with no product-related adverse events reported.

PMID: 39256350 · clinical_trial · positive · injection
Subretinal delivery of AAV8-RLBP1 gene therapy was well tolerated and significantly improved dark adaptation kinetics in all dose cohorts of patients with RLBP1-associated retinal dystrophy, with resolution of disease-related retinal deposits suggesting successful restoration of the visual cycle.

In NASH patients, hepatic ALDH1A2 and ALDH1A3 (key enzymes converting retinaldehyde to retinoic acid) were significantly underexpressed and inversely correlated with elevated plasma retinol levels, suggesting altered retinol homeostasis contributes to hepatic fibrosis progression in NAFLD.
