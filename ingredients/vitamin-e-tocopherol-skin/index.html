PMID: 15608499 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
A single wash with alpha-tocopherol-enriched rinse-off product significantly increased vitamin E levels in skin surface lipids for at least 24 hours and inhibited UVA-induced squalene photooxidation compared to control.

PMID: 15131729 · RCT · positive · mixed
Topical delivery of vitamin E via body wash was significantly more effective at increasing stratum corneum vitamin E levels (53-fold vs baseline) compared to oral dietary supplementation (8-fold vs baseline), while brief sunlight exposure depleted superficial stratum corneum vitamin E by 50-65%.

PMID: 9448204 · RCT · positive · oral
Combined oral supplementation of vitamin C (2g/day) and vitamin E (1000 IU/day) for 8 days significantly increased the minimal erythema dose and reduced cutaneous blood flow response to UV irradiation, demonstrating measurable photoprotective effects against sunburn in humans.
A randomized, double-blind placebo-controlled trial evaluating the effects of vitamin E and selenium on arsenic-induced skin lesions in Bangladesh
PMID: 16217243 · RCT · negative · oral
Six months of oral vitamin E and/or selenium supplementation produced only slight, statistically non-significant improvements in arsenical skin lesion scores compared to placebo in chronically arsenic-exposed Bangladeshi adults.
Oral supplementation with all-Rac- and RRR-alpha-tocopherol increases vitamin E levels in human sebum after a latency period of 14-21 days
PMID: 15753144 · RCT · positive · oral
Oral supplementation with 400mg of either RRR- or all-rac-alpha-tocopheryl acetate for 14 days increased facial sebum vitamin E levels by approximately 87-92% by day 21, suggesting sebaceous gland secretion is a key delivery mechanism for vitamin E bioavailability in skin, with a latency period of 2-3 weeks required for significant accumulation.
Vitamin E supplementation and in vivo immune response in healthy elderly subjects. A randomized controlled trial
PMID: 9134944 · 1997 · RCT · positive · oral
Oral vitamin E supplementation at 200 mg/day for 235 days significantly enhanced cell-mediated immunity in healthy elderly subjects, including a 65% increase in delayed-type hypersensitivity skin response and a 6-fold increase in hepatitis B antibody titers compared to placebo.

Oral supplementation with carotenoids (25 mg/day) and a combination of carotenoids plus vitamin E (335 mg/day alpha-tocopherol) significantly reduced UV-induced erythema in humans after 8 weeks, with the combination providing greater protection than carotenoids alone.

PMID: 1404449 · clinical_trial · negative · mixed
Long-term oral beta-carotene supplementation (15-60 mg/day for 9 months) significantly reduced plasma alpha-tocopherol (vitamin E) levels by up to 40% in humans and decreased skin alpha-tocopherol concentrations in UV-irradiated mice, indicating a potentially harmful nutrient interaction.
The efficacy of topical human amniotic membrane-mesenchymal stem cell-conditioned medium (hAMMSC-CM) and a mixture of topical hAMMSC-CM + vitamin C and hAMMSC-CM + vitamin E on chronic plantar ulcers
PMID: 29671368 · RCT · positive · topical
Topical hAMMSC-CM combined with vitamin E (α-tocopherol) demonstrated the greatest reduction in ulcer size and best wound healing outcomes in chronic plantar ulcers in leprosy compared to hAMMSC-CM alone or hAMMSC-CM combined with vitamin C.

PMID: 19796562 · clinical_trial · positive · topical
Topical vitamin E (D-alpha-tocopheryl acetate) gel significantly reduced digital ulcer healing time (13.22 vs 20.94 weeks), pain resolution, number of medications, and treatment costs compared to standard care alone in systemic sclerosis patients.

Oral supplementation with vitamin E (600 IU) and/or vitamin D (1600 IU) for 60 days significantly reduced SCORAD in atopic dermatitis patients, with the combination group achieving the greatest improvement of 64.3%, and plasma α-tocopherol showing a significant negative correlation with disease severity.
