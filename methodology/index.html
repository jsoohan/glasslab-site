Methodology

Brand-free evidence index, transparent brand layer.

Why we strip brand names

Brand / trade names (Botox, Thermage, Juvederm, Ultherapy, Sculptra, Profhilo, EMFACE, Renuva, MiraJet, Sofwave, etc.) are written by manufacturers and clinics, not PubMed indexers. Most cosmetic dermatology papers are indexed by mechanism, MeSH heading, or INN drug name — not the trade name.

Earlier index versions sneaked brands into queries, which (a) inflated counts for brands that happen to appear in journal abstracts and (b) excluded perfectly good RCTs that never mention the brand. v5 separates the two layers cleanly.

Brand → class mapping is its own table

239 Korean clinic brand entries are mapped to 48 generic MoA classes in brand_to_class_map.csv. 4516 procedure listings across 11 clinics are joined to MoA in clinic_procedure_to_class.csv at 90.9% coverage.

Each cell page shows which Korean brand variants and which clinics offer that MoA × indication pairing — but the underlying PubMed search uses generic terms only.

PubMed concept-AND query

({MoA concept block})
   AND
({Indication concept block})

Each concept block is a parenthesised OR of MeSH headings and [tiab] synonyms. For example a botulinum MoA block uses "Botulinum Toxins, Type A"[Mesh] alongside INN names "onabotulinumtoxinA"[tiab], "abobotulinumtoxinA"[tiab], etc., and never the trade names Botox / Dysport / Xeomin.

Paper tier system

Every fetched paper is labelled by publication type:

  • tier1 RCT, meta-analysis, systematic review
  • tier2 Clinical trial, observational, cohort, case-control
  • tier3 Case reports, letters, editorial
  • tier0 Review, practice guideline
  • other Journal article without specific type
